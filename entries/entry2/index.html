FLY TO THE SKY












In azure realms where dreams take flight,
Where day and dusk dance soft and light,
A canvas vast, where stories shroud,
Unfolds above, a tapestry, cloud.
Cotton wisps, like whispered sighs,
Brush the canvas of the skies,
They drift and weave, a fleeting crowd,
Nature's poetry sung aloud.
The sun, a painter, casts its hue,
On billowed forms, a golden brew,
At twilight, paints with hues unbowed,
A masterpiece in every cloud.
In storms, they gather, fierce and grand,
A tempest's heart, a wild command,
Yet still, within, a grace endowed,
A beauty found in tempests loud.

For in their dance, both free and vowed, Lies nature's art, in every cloud.
